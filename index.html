"He's So Lucky"

I can't speak for other parents who have gone thru the adoption process but this is one statement that I don't particularly care for. Let me give you the short list of the process.

  • PATH classes
  • Criminal background check
  • Home inspection
  • Physical
Can someone explain to me what part luck played in this? Also, there were eight (if I remember correctly) other families waiting in line to adopt and we were chosen. So if there was any "luck" involved it befell to us, not him.

"We Have a Son"

Our should I say we're caring for a male child who is a ward of the state. Once a child has been placed in your home you probably think that's the end. Actually you're somewhat on probationary status where for a minimum of six months you are "fostering" your child. You have to get approval for what doctor you can take them to. You're subject to regularly scheduled home visits from the social worker. I casually mentioned to the social worker during our plans to take our son to Little Rock, Arkansas for Christmas to visit my family. Her response "Okay, I'll issue you a travel letter." I had forgotten I had to get permission to take him out of state. Due to several missteps and mishaps our "fostering" lasted 21 months.
